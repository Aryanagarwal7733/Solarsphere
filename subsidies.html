Government Norms & Subsidies

Understand the PM Surya Ghar: Muft Bijli Yojana national program guidelines, technical rooftop standards, and bank subsidy processing.

PM Surya Ghar Subsidy Structure

The Ministry of New and Renewable Energy (MNRE) provides direct Central Financial Assistance (CFA) to residential rooftop solar installations. Subsidies are direct bank transfers based on installed capacity:

System Capacity (kW) Central Govt. Subsidy (CFA)
1 kW System ₹30,000
2 kW System ₹60,000
3 kW to 10 kW Systems ₹78,000 (Maximum Cap)
Group Housing Societies (GHS/RWA) ₹18,000 per kW (up to 500kW)

Mandatory Technical Norms

  • ✔ Domestic Content Requirement (DCR): To qualify for the subsidy, solar PV panels must be manufactured in India using domestic solar cells, and must be listed on the MNRE Approved List of Models and Manufacturers (ALMM).
  • ✔ Net Metering Integration: The solar setup must be connected to the local utility distribution grid (DISCOM) via a bidirectional Net Meter to monitor energy import and export.
  • ✔ DISCOM Sanctioned Load: The installed solar inverter capacity cannot exceed 100% of the sanctioned electrical load of your home connection (unless permitted by local state regulations).
  • ✔ Certified Vendors: Systems must be installed by a DISCOM-empanelled rooftop solar vendor in compliance with national safety codes.

Process Map to Claim Subsidy

1

Portal Registration

Register on the National Portal for Rooftop Solar and select your local electricity company (DISCOM).

2

DISCOM Feasibility Approval

Your local DISCOM will review and approve technical feasibility for rooftop installation.

3

Installation & Net Metering

The panels are installed by solarsphere's empanelled technicians, followed by Net Meter setup by the DISCOM.

4

Subsidy Release

Following joint inspection, the subsidy is directly transferred into the customer's bank account within 30 days.
